“Enhancing Virtual Reality Accessibility for Blind Users”
Main Heading 1
Subheading 1.1
Presented by Samuel Thurston • Class: English 110C, UIN: 01260919
Project Nature Slide
This individual project aims to transform virtual reality into an accessible platform for blind users. Moving beyond traditional formats, this presentation focuses on innovative approaches to make VR technology inclusive.
Topic Idea Slide
Central to this presentation is enhancing VR accessibility for the blind. Despite VR’s potential to open new worlds for those visually impaired, access barriers remain a significant challenge to be addressed.
Proposal Overview Slide
The proposal outlines strategies to integrate assistive technologies in VR, enabling blind individuals to participate fully in VR environments for various activities.
Research Paper Relevance Slide 1
Research by Maidenbaum and Amedi highlights the potential of Sensory Substitution Devices (SSDs) in VR. This aligns with our proposal’s aim to use similar technologies for enhancing VR accessibility for the blind. (Maidenbaum and Amedi).
Research Paper Relevance Slide 2
The paper’s emphasis on SSDs as tools for accessible VR environments underscores our strategy to incorporate these devices, making complex VR settings navigable for the blind. (Maidenbaum and Amedi).
Research Paper Relevance Slide 3
The motivation behind the research on SSDs resonates with our project’s aim: to transform the virtual experience for the blind from inaccessible to empowering. (Maidenbaum and Amedi).
Proposal Impact Slide
Building on the research insights, our proposal envisions a VR environment where traditional limitations are overcome, paving the way for an inclusive virtual experience for the blind.
Conclusion Slide
In conclusion, integrating ideas from existing research, this proposal envisions a future where VR is a realm of inclusivity and empowerment for the blind.
